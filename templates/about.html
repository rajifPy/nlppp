What is CERMAT?

CERMAT (Classification & Extraction of Research for Mapping Targets) is an intelligent research classification system designed to automatically identify and map research documents to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Our platform combines advanced machine learning models with rule-based detection methods to provide accurate, transparent, and comprehensive SDGs classification for academic and research institutions.

Purpose

CERMAT was developed to address the growing need for automated SDGs classification in academic research. With thousands of research papers published daily, manual classification becomes impractical and inconsistent.

Efficiency

Reduce classification time from hours to seconds

Accuracy

Maintain consistent classification standards

Organization

Systematically organize research by SDGs

Insights

Gain insights into research focus areas

How CERMAT Works

1

Input

Users upload research documents or enter title, abstract, and keywords

2

Processing

System analyzes content using AI models and/or rule-based detection

3

Classification

Documents are classified according to relevant SDGs with confidence scores

4

Results

Detailed results are provided with visualizations and explanations

5

Storage

Results are saved to history for future reference and analysis

Why CERMAT Matters

In today's research landscape, aligning work with the UN SDGs is crucial for funding, collaboration, and impact measurement. CERMAT enables:

Research Institutions

  • Track SDGs alignment across departments
  • Identify research gaps and opportunities
  • Streamline reporting for funding agencies

Researchers

  • Quickly identify relevant SDGs for publications
  • Discover related research in specific SDG areas
  • Enhance grant proposals with SDGs alignment

Policy Makers

  • Monitor research trends in specific SDG areas
  • Identify research needs for policy development
  • Evaluate research impact on sustainable development
